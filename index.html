Plataforma y propuesta de valor

PulseReport centraliza la información clínica clave para que el traspaso entre turnos sea más claro, rápido y confiable.

Problema

La información clínica suele quedar dispersa en hojas, chats o registros parciales, lo que genera omisiones y pérdida de continuidad asistencial.

Para quién

Hospitales, clínicas privadas y áreas de enfermería cardiovascular que necesitan registrar y consultar información crítica con orden.

Beneficio

Mejora la trazabilidad, reduce errores de traspaso y permite un seguimiento más seguro y oportuno del paciente.

The problem we solve

Clinical environments handle critical, time-sensitive information every day at every hour and yet the tools used to manage that information often fall short.

Manual, paper based processes

Even today many clinical handoffs still rely on handwritten notes, printed sheets, and verbal summaries that are easy to misread, lose, or overlook between shifts.

Fragmented information

In many places, patient data lives across disconnected tools such as spreadsheets, chat apps and partially filled forms that makes it difficult to get a complete, up-to-date clinical picture.

Coordination delays

When medical teams cannot quickly access shared clinical records, critical decisions slow down and ultimately the patient is the one who suffers as a result.

Loss of traceability

Without a centralized audit trail, it becomes nearly impossible to reconstruct what happened, when, and who was responsible. This is ultimately, a key gap for clinical accountability.

Discontinuity of care

Shift changes are high-risk moments. When the outgoing team cannot fully pass context to the incoming team, patient care suffers from gaps in awareness and continuity.

Hidden inefficiencies

Time spent searching for scattered records, re-entering data, or using slow/old services adds up which silently drains nursing capacity that should go directly to patients.

These are not isolated inconveniences but rather a pattern in many places. They are systemic risks embedded in how clinical teams communicate. With the well-being of many people at stake, these issues are too high to leave unaddressed.
